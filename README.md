# Bill of Quantities (BOQ) & Cost Analysis — Procurement Project

This portfolio project demonstrates how I use engineering Bill of Quantities (BOQ) documents as a procurement reference to support material sourcing, vendor comparison, cost planning, and procurement coordination for a residential construction project.

The project is based on a 3-Bedroom Residential Duplex with German Floor (Raft) Foundation in Lagos, Nigeria. It showcases my ability to work with project BOQs provided by engineers and Quantity Surveyors to develop supplier pricing comparisons, procurement schedules, and budget-focused procurement decisions.

---
## Project Overview

This project highlights my role in supporting construction procurement activities by:

- Using engineer-provided BOQs as procurement input documents
- Coordinating supplier sourcing and quotation collection
- Comparing vendor pricing across major material categories
- Supporting budget monitoring and procurement cost control
- Assisting procurement planning across construction phases
- Tracking procurement costs against project estimates

The end result is a structured procurement cost plan aligned with project scope and budget expectations.

## Procurement Workflow Applied

**1. BOQ Review for Procurement Planning**
- Review BOQ sections relevant to procurement activities
- Identify material categories and supplier requirements
- Understand procurement needs by construction phase
  
**2. Vendor Sourcing & Quotation Collection**
- Obtain quotations from multiple suppliers
- Compare unit pricing and delivery timelines
- Benchmark supplier pricing against prevailing market conditions
  
**3. Supplier Evaluation**

Suppliers were assessed based on:

- Pricing competitiveness
- Delivery capability
- Material availability
- Reliability and responsiveness
  
**4. Procurement Cost Monitoring**
- Consolidate supplier quotations into a procurement cost sheet
- Monitor estimated procurement costs by project section
- Support budget visibility and procurement decision-making
